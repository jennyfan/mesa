Designing family-centered voice interactions for a new age.

Our work pushes the boundaries of what's possible with voice-enabled artifically intelligent agents (such as Alexa or Google Home) by simulating real conversational dialogue. We make it happen through a combination of intentional, research-backed design principles and clever coding.

A novel method for traversing conversations.

Identify the meat.

It all starts with the initiation of a conversation, with any open-ended ____ from the individual. Our algorithm does the work of identifying interesting topics of the conversation -- the basis of an interesting conversation.

Dig deeper.

The algorithm uses the type of topic it identified to internally generate possible natural questions that could follow. These are meant to keep things open-ended and encourage good, rich storytelling from the human side.

Response and re-entitize.

After the user responds, we generate a new set of topics, building a tree of relationships between topics in the conversation.

Keep it flowing.

Conversations don't naturally dig deep forever. As such, we use all the context of the conversation -- the tone, the sentiment, and the presence of new topics -- to decide whether to keep digging deeper or switch to an older topic in the conversation. Of course, if it looks like there's nothing more to talk about, we'll stop pushing.

Design Principles for Voice UI Design for the Elderly

Accessibility

  • Use feedback to confirm actions and reinforce interactions
  • Account for flexible, fuzzy, and forgiving user inputs
  • Use familiar devices and affordances for user comfort with AI
  • Adapt for slow speed, loud volume, and low pitch for accessibility

Conversation

  • Develop a realistic AI persona that avoids the "uncanny valley"
  • Transition the relationship from servant to companion
  • Train your users in how to interact with your voice agent
  • Mirror human conversations for better interactions
  • Maintain and traverse a contextual conversation model

Empowerment

  • Empower the elderly with agency and choice
  • Engage family and close friends to increase adoption
  • Build trust through non-judgment and positive feedback
